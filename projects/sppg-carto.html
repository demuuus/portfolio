Badan Gizi Nasional - SPPG Carto

Overview

SPPG Carto is a data-driven geospatial platform that visualizes the nationwide distribution of SPPG (Satuan Pendidikan Penyelenggara Gizi) facilities across Indonesia. The project transforms fragmented administrative records into an interactive map, enabling users to explore coverage, analyze geographic patterns, and understand the spatial reach of nutrition programs at scale.

Problem

SPPG data was originally distributed across thousands of rows in tabular formats, making it difficult to interpret spatially. Without a geographic interface, identifying coverage gaps, clustering patterns, or regional disparities required manual analysis and offered limited insight into real-world distribution.

Solution

I designed and built a full data pipeline and visualization system that converts raw SPPG records into a geospatial dataset and renders them as an interactive map. The platform integrates data scraping, cleaning, geocoding, and enrichment processes, allowing users to view SPPG locations with additional contextual information such as contact details and online presence.

Key Features

  • Interactive map displaying 26,000+ SPPG locations across Indonesia
  • Data enrichment with contact details, websites, and social media links
  • Geographic filtering by province, city, district, and village
  • Aggregated statistics and distribution insights
  • Clean and scalable dataset prepared for further analysis or integration
  • Smooth rendering of large datasets for real-time exploration

Technical Challenges

The primary challenge was handling large-scale, inconsistent raw data. The dataset required extensive preprocessing, including resolving broken CSV structures, normalizing text fields, and ensuring consistent formatting across thousands of entries.

Geocoding accuracy was another critical issue, requiring careful batching, retry logic, and rate-limit handling to convert addresses into reliable coordinates. Additionally, rendering tens of thousands of points efficiently required optimizing the data structure and visualization pipeline to maintain performance.

Impact

This project converts static administrative data into an accessible, interactive spatial tool. It significantly reduces the effort required to analyze nationwide SPPG coverage and enables users to quickly identify patterns, gaps, and clusters that were previously difficult to detect. Beyond visualization, the dataset is now structured and enriched, making it suitable for further research, policy analysis, or system integration.
